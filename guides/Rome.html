Welcome to Rome

1. Villa Borghese

Villa Borghese is a large park in Rome that offers a peaceful escape from the city. It features several gardens, a lake, and plenty of walking trails.

2. San Luigi dei Francesi

San Luigi dei Francesi is a beautiful church in Rome that is home to three famous paintings by Caravaggio. The church is a must-visit for art lovers and those interested in history.

3. Museo Gregoriano Etrusco

The Museo Gregoriano Etrusco is a museum in Rome that is dedicated to Etruscan art and culture. It features a wide range of artifacts, including sculptures, jewelry, and pottery.

4. Tempio di Ercole Vincitore

The Tempio di Ercole Vincitore is an ancient Roman temple in Rome that is dedicated to the god Hercules. It is one of the best-preserved ancient temples in the city.

5. Piazza del Popolo

Piazza del Popolo is a large public square in Rome that is known for its beautiful fountains and architecture. It is a popular spot for both locals and tourists.
